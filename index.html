About me

Personal and academic background

I grew up in the Canary Islands, in the northeastern Atlantic, where the ocean is part of everyday life. This close connection with the sea naturally shaped my curiosity about marine environments and the processes that regulate their variability, vulnerability and role in the Earth system.

That interest led me to develop an academic path in environmental sciences, oceanography and, ultimately, chemical oceanography. Over time, my research has focused on understanding the marine carbon cycle, the carbonate system and the exchange of CO₂ between the ocean and the atmosphere, combining field observations, autonomous platforms, laboratory analyses and model-based approaches.

CV summary

Concise overview

I am a chemical oceanographer with a PhD in Oceanography and Global Change from the University of Las Palmas de Gran Canaria, defended on 5 November 2025 with Cum Laude distinction.

My work focuses on marine biogeochemical cycles, carbonate-system dynamics, ocean acidification, anthropogenic carbon storage and air–sea CO₂ exchange, with a strong emphasis on high-resolution observations from Voluntary Observing Ships, coastal campaigns and oceanographic cruises.

Since 2021, I have co-authored six peer-reviewed open-access articles in JCR-indexed Q1 journals, five of them as first author. I have also completed international research stays at IIM-CSIC and AWI, contributed to national and regional research projects, participated in international conferences, and gained substantial experience in fieldwork, autonomous observations, laboratory analyses, teaching and outreach.
